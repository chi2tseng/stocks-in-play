Business
Circle Internet Group, Inc. (NYSE: CRCL) operates as a global financial technology company providing platform, network, and market infrastructure for stablecoin and blockchain applications; it issues regulated stablecoins including USDC, a U.S. dollar-pegged digital asset, EURC, a euro-denominated stablecoin, and USYC, a tokenized money market fund backed by U.S. Treasury holdings, alongside services such as Circle Mint for minting and redeeming stablecoins at scale, Circle Payments Network for real-time global payments among financial institutions, StableFX for onchain foreign exchange, Cross-Chain Transfer Protocol for seamless USDC movement across blockchains, developer tools including wallets, smart contracts, xReserve, Gateway, and Paymaster, and Arc, an open Layer-1 blockchain for programmable money and onchain innovation. Founded in 2013 by Jeremy Allaire and Sean Neville and headquartered at One World Trade Center in New York, New York, the company holds extensive regulatory authorizations including money transmitter licenses in 46 U.S. states, MiCA compliance and electronic money institution status in the European Union, electronic money licenses in the United Kingdom, major payment institution approval in Singapore, and approvals in the United Arab Emirates, Bermuda, Canada, and Japan, enabling operations serving businesses, developers, financial institutions, and consumers across 185+ countries with deep liquidity and 24/7 settlement. Recent developments include its June 2025 initial public offering on the NYSE, the launch of the Arc public testnet with over 100 participants, expansion of Circle Payments Network to 29 enrolled banks processing $3.4 billion in annualized volume as of November 2025, strategic partnerships with Kraken to expand EURC access, Fireblocks for institutional custody and tokenization, Finastra for USDC integration into cross-border payments processing over $5 trillion daily, Brex, Deutsche Börse Group, Hyperliquid, Unibanco Itaú, and Visa, and the acquisition of Malachite's high-performance consensus engine from Informal Systems to power Arc, alongside reporting $740 million in third-quarter 2025 revenue up 66% year-over-year with USYC growing to approximately $1 billion.
